His significance

Rabbi Yehuda Leib Fishman Maimon represents the transition from Religious Zionism as a movement to Religious Zionism as a force participating in the institutions of the Yishuv and the State of Israel.

He was a public leader, writer, educator and statesman who connected Torah, Mizrachi, national institutions and the building of Jewish sovereignty.

Name etymology

  • Yehuda: A biblical Hebrew name from the root y-d-h, associated with thanks, praise and leadership.
  • Leib: A Yiddish-Ashkenazi name meaning lion, often paired with Yehuda because of the biblical phrase “Judah is a lion’s cub.”
  • Fishman: An Ashkenazi family name from Yiddish/German, literally “fish man” or fisherman.
  • Maimon: A Hebrew-Jewish name associated with Sephardi Jewish tradition and with the family name of Maimon, father of Maimonides. Fishman adopted the name Maimon as part of a broader Hebrew-national identity.

Main activity

  • Leadership in the Mizrachi movement in Eretz Israel.
  • Building Religious Zionist educational, cultural and public institutions.
  • Participation in the leadership of the Yishuv.
  • Signing the Declaration of Independence.
  • Serving as the first Minister of Religions of the State of Israel.

Historical contribution

Fishman Maimon transformed Religious Zionism into a public and state-building force. He symbolizes the movement from longing and ideology to practical responsibility within a sovereign Jewish state.
