His significance

Rabbi Shmuel Mohilever was one of the leading figures who connected the traditional rabbinic world with Hibbat Zion. Unlike earlier heralds who worked mainly on the level of ideas, Mohilever also acted as an organizer, public leader and practical supporter of settlement.

Name etymology

  • Shmuel: A biblical Hebrew name often understood as “God has heard.”
  • Mohilever: An Ashkenazi Eastern European family name. Its exact meaning is not certain, so it is best understood as a communal-family name from the Jewish world of Eastern Europe.

Main ideas and activity

  • Giving religious legitimacy to Hibbat Zion and settlement.
  • Supporting the early colonies in the Land of Israel.
  • Building bridges between rabbis, activists, donors and settlers.
  • Transforming love of Zion into practical responsibility.

The shmita controversy

Mohilever was connected to the effort to find halakhic solutions for the fragile agricultural colonies during the shmita year. This controversy showed how early Religious Zionism faced not only ideals, but difficult practical questions created by renewed Jewish agriculture in the Land of Israel.

Baron Rothschild and Ekron

One of Mohilever’s important public actions was his appeal to Baron Edmond de Rothschild for support of Jewish settlement, especially the colony of Ekron, later Mazkeret Batya. This episode symbolizes the link between rabbinic leadership, philanthropy, settlement and Hibbat Zion.
