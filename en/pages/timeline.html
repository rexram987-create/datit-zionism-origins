Main historical stages

Early 19th century

Heralds of return

Rabbis Yehuda Alkalai and Zvi Hirsch Kalischer present early religious arguments for practical return to Zion.

Late 19th century

Hibbat Zion

Rabbi Shmuel Mohilever connects rabbinic leadership with settlement, colonies and public activism.

1902

Mizrachi

Rabbi Yitzchak Yaakov Reines establishes the Mizrachi movement as an organized Religious Zionist framework.

20th century

Rav Kook

Rabbi Abraham Isaac Kook gives Religious Zionism a deep spiritual and philosophical language.

1948 and after

State of Israel

Rabbi Yehuda Leib Fishman Maimon represents the transition from movement to state institutions.
