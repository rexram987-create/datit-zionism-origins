Language and meaning

Etymology

The term “Religious Zionism” brings together two worlds: Zion, the ancient biblical and historical symbol of Jewish return, and religious life rooted in Torah, halakhah and tradition.

Zion

“Zion” originally refers to a specific place in Jerusalem, but in Jewish tradition it became a broad symbol for Jerusalem, the Land of Israel, the hope of return, and the restoration of Jewish national life.

Zionism

Zionism is the modern Jewish national movement that sought the return of the Jewish people to its ancestral homeland and the building of public, cultural, political and later sovereign life in the Land of Israel.

Religious Zionism

Religious Zionism interprets the return to Zion not only as a national or political project, but also as a religious and historical process. It links settlement, public responsibility, education and state-building with Torah, faith and the hope of redemption.

Core meaning

The phrase therefore means a movement that seeks to unite Jewish national revival with religious commitment. Its central question is how Torah, peoplehood and the Land of Israel can shape a renewed Jewish public life.
