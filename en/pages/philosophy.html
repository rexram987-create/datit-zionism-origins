Ideas and foundations

Religious Zionist Thought

The intellectual foundations of Religious Zionism connect faith, redemption, settlement, education, national responsibility and the renewal of Jewish life in the Land of Israel.

From longing to action

For centuries, Zion was the object of prayer and longing. Religious Zionist thought added a practical dimension: human initiative, immigration, settlement and public organization could be part of preparing for redemption.

Redemption and human responsibility

The early thinkers argued that faith does not cancel action. On the contrary, belief in redemption can require responsibility, planning and national effort.

Torah and national revival

Religious Zionism seeks to build Jewish national life without separating it from Torah. The national revival is expected to include education, culture, ethics, public institutions and spiritual meaning.

The Land of Israel

The Land of Israel is not merely a territory or a political refuge. It is seen as the place where the Jewish people can return to full historical, spiritual and public life.

From movement to state

As the movement developed, Religious Zionism moved from ideas and settlement activism to political organization, spiritual interpretation, institutional leadership and participation in the State of Israel.
